01 — Executive Summary

Airborne-First.
Quantitative-Grade.

The HyperSoilNet pipeline was successfully transitioned from a legacy satellite-first architecture to an airborne-focused, high-resolution spectral modeling framework. Utilising 430 native airborne hyperspectral bands and a multi-stage modeling process, the system achieved R² scores exceeding 0.90 for five of six trace element targets.

Results confirm that high-GSD aerial spectral data is highly effective for quantitative soil property mapping at precision agriculture scale.

02 — Pipeline Stages

Five Phases.
One Coherent System.

01
Preprocessing & Ingestion
01_preprocess.py
  • HSI Airborne · 430 bands · ~1.12 nm/band
  • DN → Reflectance via ÷ 10,000
  • Field boundary mask · masked mean spectrum
  • Linear interpolation → 150-band standard grid
↓
02
Deep Spectral Embedding
02_train_cnn.py
  • 1D-CNN · CBAM attention · 4-stage ResNet
  • SNV pre-normalisation before CNN
  • 350 epochs · SGDR cosine annealing
  • 128-d embedding output
↓
03
Feature Engineering
03_extract_features.py
  • Continuum Removal (CR)
  • Savitzky-Golay 1st & 2nd derivatives
  • Discrete Wavelet Transform (DWT · Meyer)
  • FFT & SVD components
↓
04
Stacked Ensemble
04_train_ensemble.py
  • XGBoost + CatBoost + Random Forest
  • SelectFromModel → top 400 features
  • NNLS per-target blending weights
  • 5-fold stratified cluster CV
↓
05
Local Residual Correction
05_local_correction.py
  • k=30 cosine-similarity spectral neighbours
  • Local LightGBM trained on residuals
  • +0.12–0.18 R² documented gain
  • Systematic bias reduced across clusters
